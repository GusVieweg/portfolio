Motivation

So I've finally gotten around to building up a website for myself, and, while I'm off of school for another couple of weeks or so, I figured it would be a good time to go ahead and start writing things out. I guess to begin with I should introduce myself. My name is Joshua Potter. I'm a computer science and math major currently at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, graduating in approximately 5 months. In my free time I like to program different things, rock climb, study, and play guitar. And that's about it...

Well alright, I don't think two sentences is adequate in describing anyone completely, let alone myself, but I have trouble organizing exactly what my "core" features are. This brings me (albeit clumsily) to my first goal of this blog. Though I may not be able to clearly explain who I am, I hope I can look back over some series of posts and perhaps at least see who I was over the course of some period of time. By having an actual forum to write out to, I anticipate more pressure in keeping this site up-to-date and, consequently, to continue writing posts out. And because this is public, I hope this forces me to write with as much clarity as possible, regardless of whether or not someone actually reads this.

Secondly, there has always been something very appealing about recording my thoughts onto something. I've tried other blogging platforms before as well as physical journals, but I found I lost interest in both fairly quickly or felt the task became a bit tedious. With the blogging platforms, I lacked control on the features I'd like to include, and the physical entries simply took far too long. I'm also a bit of a perfectionist and whenever I made a typo, I often would rewrite the entire entry. At the risk of sounding a bit corny (which I anticipate I'll sound for a considerable while), there is something romantic about the idea of recording one's thoughts. Though I clearly won't include everything I'm thinking, the notion of looking through my archives and figuring out where I was or how I felt based on the context of what I was writing is incredibly attractive. Plus, an excuse to feel like I'm being productive is always nice to have.

Site Status

Before jumping into the features that I'd like to eventually include, I think I should just quickly brush over the organization of the site. To begin with, the projects tab should be pretty self-explanatory. In the list are certain projects (either school-related or independently worked on) that I've written over the course of my computer science career. Considering one of my intentions with my overall site is to serve as a portfolio for others to be able to view my work, I deemed the projects tab should be on the front. Next, I'll explain the distinction I make between the "blog" and "research" tabs. Both will contain content much like this current post (though I'll definitely start including pictures and diagrams for all subsequent posts), but my "blog" tab will focus on content related to me personally, be it how I feel, certain philosophical arguments on my head, different musical projects I wanted to share, etc. The "research" tab will alternatively contain posts regarding certain concepts I've been exploring. For instance, I anticipate my first post being an in depth explanation on how Fourier transforms work, the applications of this, possible visualizations (probably written with Matplotlib in the backend), etc. That is, the "research" tab will contain more academically related content while the "blog" tab will contain everything else. As such, it should be expected that the "research" tab will be updated much more slowly.

Lastly, let's move on to the features I'd like to eventually include. First off, I want to eventually include a means of controlling the amount of content displayed when first loading my page. This will be pretty simple (basic AJAX calls), but I mention this because, as of the time of this writing, there is no need to include them. Secondly, once content begins growing, I'd like to include options on how posts should be displayed. My projects list in particular may be a bit difficult to navigate, but perhaps an optional means of displaying a grid instead would prove more useful. Third, I'd like to incorporate Pandoc and write my posts in Markdown, converting them to HTML via some pipeline when saving posts. I still have a lot more I'd like to add, but these will probably take priority, and I'll include another update once these changes are in effect.

Conclusion

These "meta" posts regarding the status of the site itself should be far and in between. But as an initial post I thought it would be appropriate to include. How frequently I'll update this site with less technical posts I'm not sure, but I hope it'll be relatively often for my own sake. If no updates occur from this point onward, thanks for taking the time to read this post. But if not, please feel free to keep following.

Until next time!
